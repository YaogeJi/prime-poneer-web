abstract: We study linear regression from data distributed over a network of agents (with no master node) by means of LASSO estimation, in high-dimension, which allows the ambient dimension to grow faster than the sample size. This paper provides a first statistical study of the Distributed Gradient Descent (DGD) in the Adapt-Then-Combine (ATC) form. Our theory shows that DGD-ATC converges globally at a linear rate to an estimate that is within the centralized statistical precision of the model.
